## Recommended Legal Structure

### Parent Company: Denvan Holdings LLC
- **Entity Type:** Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- **State of Formation:** [Your state - recommend Delaware or home state]
- **Tax Election:** Consult CPA -- options include:
  - Default LLC (pass-through)
  - S-Corp election (Form 2553) for tax optimization once revenue supports it
  - C-Corp election if seeking outside investment
- **Registered Agent:** Required in state of formation
- **Operating Agreement:** Multi-member or single-member operating agreement required

### Subsidiaries: Two Options

#### Option A: DBAs (Doing Business As) -- Simplest
- Filed under Denvan Holdings LLC
- Share the parent company's EIN and liability
- Best for: Early-stage subsidiaries, low-risk businesses, testing new ventures
- **Pros:** Simple, low cost, shared accounting
- **Cons:** No liability separation between DBAs

#### Option B: Separate LLCs -- Recommended for Growth
- Each subsidiary is its own LLC, owned by Denvan Holdings LLC
- Each has its own EIN, bank account, and operating agreement
- Best for: Established businesses, higher-risk operations, investor-backed ventures
- **Pros:** Liability protection, clean financials, easier to sell or spin off
- **Cons:** More paperwork, separate tax filings, higher cost

### Recommended Approach
**Start with DBAs, graduate to separate LLCs as each subsidiary grows.**

| Stage | Structure | When to Use |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Testing/Launch | DBA under Denvan Holdings | Revenue < $50K/year |
| Growth | Separate LLC owned by Holdings | Revenue > $50K/year or higher risk |
| Mature | Separate LLC with own team | Revenue > $250K/year, own employees |

## EIN and Banking Structure

### EIN Structure

| Entity | EIN | Purpose |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Denvan Holdings LLC | Primary EIN | Parent company operations, shared services |
| Subsidiary DBA 1 | Uses parent EIN | Operates under parent umbrella |
| Subsidiary LLC 1 | Own EIN | Independent operations, own tax filing |
| Subsidiary LLC 2 | Own EIN | Independent operations, own tax filing |

### Banking Structure

#### Parent Company Account
- **Primary Business Checking** - Operating expenses, shared service costs, management fees
- **Primary Business Savings** - Emergency fund, capital reserves (3-6 months operating costs)
- **Tax Savings Account** - Set aside 25-30% of net income quarterly

#### Subsidiary Accounts (per subsidiary)
- **Subsidiary Checking** - Day-to-day operations, revenue deposits, expenses
- **Subsidiary Savings** - Subsidiary-specific reserves

#### Recommended Banks
- **Mercury** - Modern business banking, good for multi-entity management
- **Relay** - Designed for multiple accounts and profit-first banking
- **Chase Business** - Traditional option with branch access
- **Novo** - Free business checking with integrations

#### Inter-Company Transactions
- Document all transfers between parent and subsidiaries
- Use management fee agreements (typically 5-15% of subsidiary revenue)
- Keep clean records for tax purposes
- Use accounting software that supports multi-entity (QuickBooks, Xero, or Wave)

## Guidelines for Adding Subsidiaries

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Identify Opportunity**
   - Market research and validation
   - Financial projections (12-month minimum)
   - Alignment with Denvan Holdings portfolio strategy

2. **Legal Setup**
   - Decide: DBA or separate LLC
   - File DBA with county/state OR form new LLC
   - Obtain EIN if separate LLC
   - Draft operating agreement (if LLC)

3. **Financial Setup**
   - Open subsidiary bank account
   - Set up accounting in parent's bookkeeping system
   - Establish management fee structure
   - Create subsidiary budget

4. **Branding Setup**
   - Apply Denvan Holdings brand guidelines (see Branding Guide)
   - Create subsidiary logo using parent brand framework
   - Set up subsidiary email addresses
   - Create subsidiary web presence (subdomain or section on main site)

5. **Operations Setup**
   - Define service offerings and pricing
   - Create standard operating procedures
   - Set up required tools and software
   - Hire or assign team members

6. **Launch**
   - Soft launch to test market
   - Gather feedback and iterate
   - Full launch with marketing support from parent company
