## Section 2: Financial Due Diligence

### Revenue Analysis
- [ ] Obtain 3 years of tax returns
- [ ] Obtain 3 years of Profit & Loss statements
- [ ] Obtain 3 years of balance sheets
- [ ] Review bank statements (12 months minimum)
- [ ] Verify revenue is real (not inflated or one-time)
- [ ] Identify revenue concentration (top 5 customers as % of total)
- [ ] Analyze revenue trends (growing, flat, declining?)
- [ ] Understand seasonality patterns

### Expense Analysis
- [ ] List all recurring expenses
- [ ] Identify owner perks/personal expenses running through business
- [ ] Calculate true owner's discretionary earnings (SDE)
- [ ] Identify expenses that would change under new ownership
- [ ] Review all vendor contracts and terms

### Assets & Liabilities
- [ ] List all physical assets (equipment, inventory, vehicles)

## Section 4: Operational Assessment

### Customer Analysis
- [ ] Who are the top 10 customers?
- [ ] Are there contracts or are relationships informal?
- [ ] How dependent is the business on the current owner's relationships?
- [ ] What is the customer retention rate?
- [ ] What is the customer acquisition cost?
- [ ] Are there recurring revenue contracts?

### Employee Assessment
- [ ] List all employees with roles, tenure, and compensation
- [ ] Which employees are critical to operations?
- [ ] Will employees stay after acquisition?
- [ ] Are there any employment contracts or obligations?
- [ ] Review any non-compete or non-solicitation agreements

### Operations
- [ ] Document all key business processes
- [ ] Identify systems and tools currently in use
- [ ] Assess how dependent operations are on the current owner
- [ ] Review any required licenses, permits, or certifications
- [ ] Understand supply chain and vendor relationships

---

## Section 5: Legal Review

- [ ] Review all active contracts (customer, vendor, lease, service)
- [ ] Check for any pending or threatened litigation
- [ ] Verify business licenses and permits are current
- [ ] Review intellectual property (trademarks, patents, copyrights)
- [ ] Check for any regulatory compliance issues
- [ ] Review insurance policies
- [ ] Verify tax compliance (no unpaid taxes or penalties)
- [ ] Review any non-compete agreements that affect the business

## Section 9: Post-Acquisition Integration Checklist

Once the deal closes:

### Week 1
- [ ] Transfer ownership of all assets
- [ ] Set up DBA under Denvan Holdings (if asset purchase)
- [ ] Open subsidiary bank account
- [ ] Set up in QuickBooks as new Class
- [ ] Announce transition to employees
- [ ] Announce transition to key customers

### Weeks 2-4
- [ ] Migrate financial operations to shared services
- [ ] Set up management fee billing
- [ ] Transition vendor relationships
- [ ] Begin customer outreach and relationship building
- [ ] Document all processes and create SOPs

### Months 2-3
- [ ] Complete migration to Denvan tools and systems
- [ ] Evaluate and optimize operations
- [ ] Implement marketing strategy
- [ ] Set first quarter KPIs and targets
- [ ] Conduct first monthly performance review
