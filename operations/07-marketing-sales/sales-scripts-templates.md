## Follow-Up Email Sequence (7-Touch Framework)

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Message |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Initial outreach (above templates) |
| 2 | Day 3 | Email | Brief follow-up: "Just checking if you saw my note" |
| 3 | Day 7 | LinkedIn | Connect request with personal note |
| 4 | Day 10 | Email | Share a relevant article or insight |
| 5 | Day 14 | Phone | Brief voicemail + email: "Tried to reach you" |
| 6 | Day 21 | Email | Different angle or new value proposition |
| 7 | Day 30 | Email | "Break-up" email: "Should I close your file?" |

### Follow-Up #2 Template
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [NAME],

Just following up on my previous message. I understand
you're busy -- would a 10-minute call work better?

Happy to work around your schedule.

[YOUR NAME]
```

### Follow-Up #7 (Break-Up) Template
```
Subject: Should I close your file?

Hi [NAME],

I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back,
which is totally fine. I don't want to be a bother.

If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. If things
change in the future, I'm always happy to chat.

I'll close out my follow-ups for now, but feel free to
reach out anytime.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## Phone Scripts

### Inbound Call: General Inquiry

```
"Thank you for calling Denvan Holdings. This is [YOUR NAME],
how can I help you today?"

[Listen]

"Great question. Let me make sure I understand -- you're
interested in [restate their need]. Is that right?"

[Confirm]

"Absolutely. [Answer their question or explain next steps].
Can I get your email so I can send you some additional
information?"

[Collect info]

"Perfect. I'll send that over today. Is there anything else
I can help with?"

"Thank you for reaching out, [NAME]. We'll be in touch soon.
Have a great day."
```

### Outbound Call: Discovery / Sales Call

```
"Hi [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] from Denvan Holdings.
Thanks for taking the time to chat.

Before I tell you about us, I'd love to learn more about
your situation. Can you tell me about [their business/need]?"

[Listen -- take notes]

"That makes sense. What's the biggest challenge you're
facing with [relevant topic]?"

[Listen]

"Got it. Here's how we typically help with that...
[Explain relevant service/solution -- keep it concise]

We've seen companies in similar situations [achieve specific
results]. Would that kind of outcome be valuable for you?"

[Listen]

"Great. Here's what I'd suggest as a next step: [propose
next action -- meeting, proposal, trial, etc.]. Does that
work for you?"

[Confirm and schedule]

"Perfect. I'll send a calendar invite and a brief overview
of what we discussed. Looking forward to it, [NAME]."
```

## Proposal Template Outline

### Cover Page
- Denvan Holdings LLC logo
- Proposal title: "Proposal for [CLIENT NAME]"
- Prepared by: [YOUR NAME], [TITLE]
- Date: [DATE]

### Section 1: Understanding Your Needs (1 page)
- Restate the client's situation and challenges
- Show you understand their goals

### Section 2: Our Solution (1-2 pages)
- Describe the specific services/approach
- How it addresses their challenges
- What makes our approach different

### Section 3: Scope of Work (1 page)
- Detailed deliverables
- Timeline with milestones
- What's included / what's not included

### Section 4: Investment (1 page)
- Pricing (clear, simple)
- Payment terms
- What they get at each price point (if tiered)

### Section 5: About Denvan Holdings (1 page)
- Brief company overview
- Relevant experience / results
- Why clients trust us

### Section 6: Next Steps
- How to accept
- Contact information
- Expiration date of proposal

## Elevator Pitches

### 30-Second Version
"Denvan Holdings is a multi-industry holding company. We build and manage businesses across different sectors under one corporate umbrella. We provide shared services -- accounting, legal, marketing, IT -- so each business can focus on serving customers while we handle the corporate infrastructure."

### 2-Minute Version
"Denvan Holdings is a holding company based in Austin, Texas. We own and operate multiple businesses across diverse industries using a DBA subsidiary model.

Here's the problem we solve: most small businesses fail not because their product is bad, but because they can't afford proper accounting, legal support, marketing, and strategic management. We provide all of that through centralized shared services.

Each subsidiary pays a management fee and gets access to our full corporate infrastructure -- the same quality of support that large corporations have, at a fraction of the cost.

Our portfolio is diversified across multiple industries, which reduces risk and creates synergies between businesses. We're always looking for new opportunities to launch or acquire businesses that align with our vision.

If you know anyone who's building a business and needs stronger corporate backing, or investors interested in a diversified portfolio, I'd love to connect."
