## OPERATING AGREEMENT OF DENVAN HOLDINGS LLC

### A Texas Limited Liability Company

---

**THIS OPERATING AGREEMENT** is made and entered into effective as of [DATE], by and among the Member(s) listed herein.

---

## ARTICLE 1: FORMATION

**1.1 Formation.** The Company was formed as a Texas Limited Liability Company by filing a Certificate of Formation with the Texas Secretary of State on [FORMATION DATE].

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is **Denvan Holdings LLC**.

**1.3 Principal Office.** The principal office of the Company is located at:
5900 Balcones Drive, STE 100, Austin, TX 78731

**1.4 Registered Agent.** The registered agent for the Company is:
[REGISTERED AGENT NAME]
[REGISTERED AGENT ADDRESS]

**1.5 Term.** The Company shall continue in existence perpetually unless dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or by law.

---

## ARTICLE 2: PURPOSE AND POWERS

**2.1 Purpose.** The purpose of the Company is to:
- Own, manage, and operate subsidiary businesses across multiple industries
- Provide management, consulting, and shared services to subsidiary entities
- Acquire, hold, and dispose of business interests and assets
- Engage in any lawful business activity permitted under Texas law

**2.2 Powers.** The Company shall have all powers available to a limited liability company under the Texas Business Organizations Code, including but not limited to:
- Opening and maintaining bank accounts
- Entering into contracts and agreements
- Hiring employees and independent contractors
- Acquiring real and personal property
- Registering and operating DBAs (Doing Business As)
- Borrowing money and granting security interests
- Investing in or acquiring other businesses

---

## ARTICLE 3: MEMBERS

**3.1 Initial Member(s).**

| Member Name | Ownership % | Capital Contribution |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| [MEMBER NAME] | [100]% | $[AMOUNT] |

**3.2 Admission of New Members.** New Members may be admitted only with the unanimous written consent of all existing Members and upon execution of an amendment to this Agreement.

**3.3 Membership Interests.** Membership interests may not be transferred, assigned, or pledged without the prior written consent of all Members.

---

## ARTICLE 4: MANAGEMENT

**4.1 Management by Members.** The Company shall be **member-managed**. All Members shall have the right to participate in the management and control of the Company's business.

**4.2 Managing Member.** [MEMBER NAME] is designated as the Managing Member with authority to:
- Execute contracts and agreements on behalf of the Company
- Open and manage bank accounts
- Hire and terminate employees and contractors
- Make day-to-day operational decisions
- Register DBAs and launch subsidiary businesses
- Manage subsidiary operations and performance

**4.3 Major Decisions.** The following decisions require the approval of Members holding at least [PERCENTAGE]% of membership interests:
- Sale or disposition of substantially all Company assets
- Merger with or acquisition of another entity
- Admission of a new Member
- Amendment of this Operating Agreement
- Dissolution of the Company
- Borrowing in excess of $[AMOUNT]
- Any single expenditure exceeding $[AMOUNT]

**4.4 Officers.** The Managing Member may appoint officers with titles and duties as determined by the Managing Member:
- Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Chief Operating Officer (COO)
- Secretary

---

## ARTICLE 5: CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

**5.1 Initial Contributions.** Each Member shall contribute the capital set forth in Section 3.1.

**5.2 Additional Contributions.** No Member shall be required to make additional capital contributions without their consent.

**5.3 Capital Accounts.** A separate capital account shall be maintained for each Member, increased by contributions and allocated profits, and decreased by distributions and allocated losses.

**5.4 No Interest on Capital.** No Member shall be entitled to interest on their capital contributions.

---

## ARTICLE 6: ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**6.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses.** Net profits and losses shall be allocated to Members in proportion to their respective membership interests.

**6.2 Distributions.** Distributions shall be made at such times and in such amounts as determined by the Managing Member, provided that:
- The Company maintains adequate cash reserves for operations
- All current obligations and expenses are met
- A minimum operating reserve of $[AMOUNT] is maintained
- Distributions are made in proportion to membership interests

**6.3 Tax Distributions.** The Company shall distribute to each Member, at least quarterly, an amount sufficient to cover the estimated income tax liability arising from the Member's share of Company income.

**6.4 Subsidiary Revenue.** Revenue from subsidiary businesses (DBAs) operated under the Company shall be treated as Company revenue and allocated according to this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE 7: ACCOUNTING AND RECORDS

**7.1 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year (January 1 - December 31).

**7.2 Books and Records.** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records including:
- Financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Tax returns and supporting documents
- Bank statements and reconciliations
- Contracts, agreements, and legal documents
- Meeting minutes and resolutions
- Records for each subsidiary business (DBA)

**7.3 Tax Returns.** The Company shall file all required federal, state, and local tax returns. As a [single-member LLC / multi-member LLC], the Company shall be treated as a [disregarded entity / partnership] for federal tax purposes unless an election is made otherwise.

**7.4 Tax Matters Member.** [MEMBER NAME] is designated as the Tax Matters Member responsible for all tax-related decisions and filings.

**7.5 Banking.** All Company funds shall be deposited in accounts in the Company's name. The Managing Member is authorized to open accounts, make deposits, and sign checks.

---

## ARTICLE 8: SUBSIDIARY BUSINESSES (DBAs)

**8.1 Authority.** The Managing Member has full authority to register, operate, and dissolve DBA (Doing Business As) entities under the Company.

**8.2 DBA Operations.** Each DBA subsidiary shall:
- Operate under the legal umbrella of Denvan Holdings LLC
- Maintain separate financial tracking within the Company's books
- Follow Company policies, brand guidelines, and operating procedures
- Pay a management fee to the Company as defined in its Management Agreement

**8.3 Management Fees.** Each subsidiary shall pay a management fee to the parent company of [10-15]% of gross revenue, as defined in the individual Management Services Agreement for that subsidiary.

**8.4 Conversion to Separate Entity.** The Managing Member may, at their discretion, convert any DBA subsidiary into a separate LLC owned by the Company if liability protection or operational needs require it.

---

## ARTICLE 9: TRANSFER OF INTERESTS

**9.1 Restrictions.** No Member may sell, assign, transfer, or pledge their membership interest without the prior written consent of all other Members.

**9.2 Right of First Refusal.** Before any transfer, the remaining Members shall have the right of first refusal to purchase the interest at fair market value.

**9.3 Involuntary Transfer.** In the event of death, disability, bankruptcy, or divorce of a Member, the remaining Members shall have the option to purchase the interest at fair market value within 90 days.

---

## ARTICLE 10: DISSOLUTION

**10.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon:
- The unanimous written consent of all Members
- Entry of a judicial decree of dissolution
- Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue

**10.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution:
1. Company assets shall be used to pay all debts and obligations
2. Remaining assets shall be distributed to Members in proportion to capital accounts
3. A Certificate of Termination shall be filed with the Texas Secretary of State

---

## ARTICLE 11: INDEMNIFICATION

**11.1 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless each Member, manager, and officer from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from their good-faith actions on behalf of the Company, except for fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence.

---

## ARTICLE 12: MISCELLANEOUS

**12.1 Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by the written consent of Members holding at least [PERCENTAGE]% of membership interests.

**12.2 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.

**12.3 Severability.** If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**12.4 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Members and supersedes all prior agreements.

**12.5 Dispute Resolution.** Any disputes shall be resolved through mediation, and if necessary, binding arbitration in Travis County, Texas.
