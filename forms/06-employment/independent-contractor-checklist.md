## 4. Texas-Specific Considerations

### Texas Workforce Commission (TWC)
- TWC may reclassify contractors as employees during audits
- TWC uses its own test (similar to IRS factors) to determine classification
- Misclassification can trigger liability for unemployment taxes, penalties, and interest

### Texas Payday Law
- The Texas Payday Law (Texas Labor Code Chapter 61) generally applies to employees, not independent contractors
- However, if a contractor is later reclassified as an employee, Payday Law protections apply retroactively

### Non-Compete Agreements in Texas
- Texas Business & Commerce Code 15.50-15.52
- Non-competes with contractors are enforceable only if they meet the "ancillary to an otherwise enforceable agreement" requirement
- Must be reasonable in time, geographic area, and scope

## 5. Misclassification Penalties

| Agency | Penalty |
|--------|---------|
| **IRS** | 100% of employer FICA taxes not withheld, plus penalties and interest; possible 1.5% of wages for income tax not withheld |
| **DOL** | Back wages, overtime, benefits; liquidated damages |
| **TWC** | Unemployment taxes, penalties, interest |
| **Workers' Comp** | Liability for workplace injuries if contractor is reclassified |
