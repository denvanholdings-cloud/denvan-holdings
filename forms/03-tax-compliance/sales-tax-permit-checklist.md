## 1. Do You Need a Sales Tax Permit?

You must obtain a Texas Sales and Use Tax Permit if:

- [ ] You sell tangible personal property in Texas
- [ ] You sell taxable services in Texas (e.g., data processing, security services, debt collection, credit reporting, insurance services, internet access, telecommunications)
- [ ] You lease or rent tangible personal property in Texas
- [ ] You sell digital products (software, streaming, digital codes)

**If the holding company only manages subsidiaries and does not directly sell taxable goods/services, a permit may not be required for the parent entity.** Each subsidiary that sells taxable goods/services will need its own permit.

## 3. Applying for a Sales Tax Permit

### Online (Recommended)
1. [ ] Go to https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/permit/
2. [ ] Click "Apply for a Sales Tax Permit"
3. [ ] Complete Form AP-201 online
4. [ ] No filing fee (free)
5. [ ] Permit typically issued within 2-3 weeks
6. [ ] Save confirmation: [CONFIRMATION NUMBER]

### By Mail
1. [ ] Download Form AP-201 from Comptroller website
2. [ ] Mail to: Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts, 111 E. 17th Street, Austin, TX 78774-0100
3. [ ] No filing fee

## 5. Filing Sales Tax Returns

### WebFile (Recommended)
1. [ ] Go to https://webfile.cpa.texas.gov
2. [ ] Log in with WebFile number
3. [ ] Report total sales, taxable sales, and tax collected
4. [ ] Remit tax due
5. [ ] Save confirmation number

### Timely Filing Discount
- File and pay on time to receive a **0.5% discount** on tax due (max $25 per month for monthly filers)

---

## 6. Resale & Exemption Certificates

### Texas Resale Certificate (Form 01-339)
- Use when purchasing items that will be resold
- [ ] Provide completed Form 01-339 to vendor
- [ ] Retain copies of all resale certificates given to you
- [ ] Resale certificates do not expire but must be updated if info changes

### Exemption Certificates
- Certain buyers may present exemption certificates (nonprofits, government, manufacturers)
- [ ] Verify exemption certificate is properly completed
- [ ] Retain exemption certificates for **4 years** from date of sale

---

## 7. Record Keeping

Retain the following for a minimum of **4 years**:

- [ ] Sales tax returns (filed copies)
- [ ] Sales invoices and receipts
- [ ] Purchase invoices
- [ ] Resale certificates received from customers
- [ ] Exemption certificates received from customers
- [ ] General ledger and journal entries
- [ ] Bank statements reconciled to sales records

## 8. Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late filing (1-30 days) | 5% of tax due |
| Late filing (31+ days) | 10% of tax due |
| Failure to obtain permit | Class C misdemeanor; fines up to $500 |
| Failure to collect tax | Responsible for uncollected tax plus penalties |
| Fraud | Criminal penalties possible |
